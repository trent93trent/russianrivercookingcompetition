About Chef Jun

Jun Summer has built a reputation for transforming familiar comfort foods into extraordinary culinary experiences. Growing up in a multigenerational household where cooking was the center of family life, Jun developed a deep appreciation for the emotional power of food and the traditions behind beloved recipes.

After studying at the Culinary Institute of America, Jun worked in high-end restaurants across the country but found the most joy in reimagining the homestyle dishes that first inspired a love of cooking. Jun's approach combines classical training with a genuine respect for traditional techniques, creating dishes that feel both nostalgic and surprising.

What sets Jun's cooking apart is an uncanny ability to preserve the soul of familiar dishes while elevating them with refined techniques and thoughtful ingredient substitutions. Each creation tells a story of cultural heritage, personal memory, and culinary innovation.

When not in the kitchen, Jun can be found collecting vintage cookbooks, hosting community cooking classes, or collaborating with local farmers to showcase heirloom varieties of fruits and vegetables in modern interpretations of classic dishes.

Signature Dishes

  • Grandmother's Garden
    Heirloom tomato pie with caramelized onion soubise, fresh herbs, and edible flowers from Jun's garden
  • Sunday Memories
    24-hour braised short rib with root vegetable puree, pickled mustard seeds, and bone marrow crust
  • Childhood Remix
    Elevated mac and cheese with five artisanal cheeses, black truffle, and crispy pancetta breadcrumbs
  • Heritage Harvest
    Seasonal vegetable pot pie with flaky herb crust, heirloom bean puree, and preserved lemon gremolata
